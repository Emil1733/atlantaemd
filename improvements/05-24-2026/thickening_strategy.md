## 🧬 Semantic Growth Architecture (Theory of Dominance)

To ensure the newly added content is highly relevant and avoids "SEO fluff," all expanded content will be built around the **4 Technical Nodes** of our **Theory of Dominance** (outlined in the `growth_playbook.md`):

1.  **Material Physics:** We will document the structural realities of pool demolition. This includes explaining the thickness of gunite pools, the layout of steel reinforcing rebar grids, the tonnage calculations for standard residential pools (typically 60 to 80 tons of masonry debris), and the process of concrete fracturing.
2.  **Machinery Specs:** We will provide deep technical reviews of the equipment we deploy. We will detail our compact excavators, mini-skid steers (specifically models designed with a 36-inch width profile to negotiate tight garden gates), hydraulic breakers, and pneumatic drills. This builds technical trust with search crawlers and eases homeowner anxiety regarding yard damage.
3.  **Soil Engineering:** We will add deep-dive explanations of the soil backfilling process. This includes describing the differences between local soil profiles (such as Cobb red clay versus DeKalb micaceous silt), moisture-control mechanics during compaction, the standard Proctor compaction test, and the requirements for structural engineered fill certification (crucial for future building or property resale).
4.  **Institutional Trust:** We will elaborate on local regulatory compliance, including the specific permit acquisition phases, arborist reviews (critical in high-canopy zones like Sandy Springs and Decatur), utility markings (811 protocols), sewer line capping, and stormwater runoff mitigation (silt fencing and erosion logs).

## 🧱 The Modular Content Blocks

To expand these pages efficiently while ensuring each page has unique, localized details, we have designed **five core content modules**. Each module will be customized for the target city or county before injection.

### Module A: Geotechnical Compaction & Backfill Mechanics (400–500 words)
This module addresses the critical phase of pool backfilling, shifting the narrative from a simple "fill-dirt" job to a professional geotechnical operation.
*   **Focus Areas:** Standard Proctor Density testing, structural stability, preventing ground subsidence, water table pressure, and the choice of backfill materials (crushed granite, gravel drainage layers, and sandy clay).
*   **Keywords:** *Proctor density, structural backfill, soil settlement, geotechnical certification, lift-by-lift compaction, moisture content, load-bearing capacity.*

### Module B: Heavy Machinery Specs & Tight-Access Logistics (350–450 words)
This module details the physical logistics of getting heavy machinery into standard suburban yards without damaging driveways, mature trees, or retaining walls.
*   **Focus Areas:** Crane access, mini-excavator width limits (36-inch gate clearances), turf protection mats, hydraulic impact hammer specifications, and vibration control near older home foundations.
*   **Keywords:** *Mini-excavator, gate clearance, turf protection, hydraulic breaker, vibration monitoring, residential access constraints, driveway load limits.*

### Module C: Municipal Permit & Structural Code Alignment (300–400 words)
This module provides a detailed walkthrough of the local municipal building codes, permit fees, inspections, and utility coordination.
*   **Focus Areas:** Permit office interactions, site plan requirements, utility capping (water, gas, electricity), arborist review for critical root zones (CRZs), and the final inspection checklist.
*   **Keywords:** *Building permit, plumbing permit, 811 Call Before You Dig, utility capping, arborist review, critical root zone, final inspection sign-off.*

### Module D: Eco-Friendly Material Recycling & Tonnage Economics (250–350 words)
This module discusses the environmental impact and cost breakdown of pool removal, highlighting concrete recycling and carbon footprint minimization.
*   **Focus Areas:** Calculations of total concrete and steel tonnage, hauling logistics, recycling concrete into road base aggregates, and diverting debris from local Georgia landfills.
*   **Keywords:** *Debris tonnage, concrete recycling, road base aggregate, waste diversion, diesel logistics, eco-friendly demolition.*

### Module E: Hydrological Engineering & Stormwater Runoff Mitigation (250–350 words)
This module explains the hydrological impact of replacing a large impermeable pool structure with soil, detailing how to manage runoff.
*   **Focus Areas:** Stormwater runoff patterns, grading plans, silt fencing, erosion control logs, French drain installation, and preventing runoff from flooding neighboring properties.
*   **Keywords:** *Stormwater runoff, grading plan, silt fence, erosion control, French drain, surface drainage, impermeable surface.*

## 🛡️ Quality Control & Implementation Rules

To maintain high visual standards and avoid layout breaking, we will adhere to the following rules during the implementation phase:

1.  **Strict File Boundaries:** Do not make any edits to the high-authority files (e.g., Duluth, Buford, Suwanee, Lawrenceville, Snellville, Peachtree City).
2.  **HTML Structure Preservation:** All injected content must be wrapped in standard, semantic HTML5 tags (`<section>`, `<h2>`, `<h3>`, `<p>`, `<ul>`, `<li>`) that match the styling architecture defined in `assets/styles.css`.
3.  **Prevent CSS Bloat:** Do not write inline styles. Use the pre-existing grid system (`.grid-2`, `.grid-3`), card components (`.card`), and button classes (`.btn`, `.btn-outline`).
4.  **No Placeholders:** All content must be fully written, containing real-world local facts. Absolutely no "Lorem Ipsum" or "Insert City Name Here" templates.
5.  **Preserve Site Features:** Ensure that the interactive elements (such as the Cost Calculator forms, sticky CTAs, and unified header/footers) remain fully functional on every page.
6.  **Code Integrity:** Run the local integrity verification script `node verify_integrity.js` and word-count checks `node scratch/authority_audit.js` after every batch of edits.
