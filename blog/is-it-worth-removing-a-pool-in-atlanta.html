Is It Worth Removing a Pool in Atlanta?

Published April 4, 2026 | By Atlanta Pool Removal Pros Team

Many homeowners in the Atlanta metropolitan area find themselves asking a critical question: "Is removing my pool worth the investment?" Whether you're in Sandy Springs, Decatur, or Marietta, the decision to remove a pool is often a combination of financial practicality and a desire for more usable space.

In 2026, the cost of pool maintenance—including electricity, chemicals, and staffing for regular cleanings—has reached new levels. For many, the pool that was once a source of joy has become a source of frustration and liability. Here’s a closer look at the factors that tilt the scales toward removal.

The Financial Impact: Ongoing Maintenance vs. One-Time Removal

While the upfront cost of pool removal can seem significant, it’s a one-time expense. Contrast this with the annual maintenance totals that can easily exceed $3,000 for a standard inground pool when you factor in repairs and operational costs. For most Atlanta homeowners, the demolition pays for itself within three to four years.

Beyond the direct savings, there’s the impact on property value. While a well-maintained pool *can* be an asset, an aging, cracked, or poorly functioning one is a major deterrent for buyers. In many cases, removing the pool and restoring the yard—ensuring proper soil compaction and drainage—can actually increase the pool of potential buyers and clarify the property's potential.

Reclaiming Your Backyard

We’ve seen firsthand how a pool removal transforms a property. Many of our clients are surprised at how much larger their lots feel once the pool and all its safety fences are gone. This reclaimed space becomes a blank canvas for gardens, patios, or even guest houses of the future.

For homeowners with kids or pets, the safety factor of removing a liability like an unused pool is immense. We take pride in delivering a clean, level surface that's ready for any new use you can imagine.

A high-value yard restoration in Sandy Springs, GA.

Ultimately, if you aren't using the pool regularly, the time, money, and space it consumes are likely worth more to you in other forms. Our team handles everything from utility disconnects to the final grading to ensure the process is seamless.
