Deep Removal vs. GA Legal Abandonment

Full Demolition (Deep Removal)

The entire pool shell is professionally broken up and hauled off-site. This is the gold standard for property value and future foundation stability in Buckhead and Marietta.

  • 100% structural integrity regained
  • Eliminates all future sinkhole risk
  • Compaction tested for future construction

Pool Abandonment (Partial)

We break the bottom and top 18-24 inches of the shell for drainage. A lower-cost alternative that is fully compliant with Fulton and DeKalb County demolition codes.

  • Fast 72-hour project completion
  • Cost-effective for non-building areas
  • Fully permitted & legal in North Georgia

Atlanta Pool Removal Permits by County

Navigating North Georgia excavation permits requires local expertise. Permit requirements vary significantly between Fulton and DeKalb County. For example, most Atlanta pool removal permits fall between $150–$350 depending on county zoning and grading requirements.

  • ✔ Fulton County: Requires specific demolition site plans for in-ground shell destruction.
  • ✔ DeKalb County: Focuses heavily on sediment control and seasonal grading plans.
  • ✔ Cobb County: Enforces engineered backfill standards for all residential demolition.

Understanding Northern Georgia Compaction

The biggest risk in pool removal is settlement. Because Atlanta’s soil profile is dense with red clay, simple backfilling leads to air pockets and uneven settling over 5-10 years. We guide homeowners toward an full pool demolition process—a method of layering soil with specific moisture control to match the surrounding virgin ground.
