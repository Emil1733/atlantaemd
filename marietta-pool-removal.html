The 10-Step Operational Workflow for Marietta Estates

1. Utility & Buffer Mapping: Identification of all gas, water, and sewer lines, including the mandatory arborist review for Historic District TPZs (Tree Protection Zones).

2. Low-Impact Ingress: Installation of 1.5-inch thick polymer mats to bridge driveways and protect mature lawns from the 20-ton load of our excavators.

3. Hydrostatic Perforation: Utilizing hydraulic breakers to drill 18-inch diameter relief holes every 4 feet through the gunite floor, ensuring permanent hydrologic flow.

4. Surgical Bond Beam Extraction: Removal of the top 36 inches of concrete, rebar, and tile. We never bury visible debris near the finish horizon.

5. The "Aggregate Sink" Layer: Placement of 18 inches of #57 washed stone at the basin floor to facilitate rapid groundwater evacuation.

6. Certified Structural Backfill: Importing quarry-certified structural fill in 6-inch lifts. Each lift is mechanically compacted using 12,000lb-frequency vibratory rollers.

7. Modified Proctor Verification: For buildable sites (ADUs/Pool Houses), we perform nuclear density testing to verify 95% compaction—the institutional gold standard.

8. Hydraulic Grading Swales: Establishing the final surface grade to redirect all stormwater toward established municipal drainage points, preventing erosion.

9. Soil Horizon Amending: Treating the final 12 inches of fill with University of Georgia-approved lime and organic compost to neutralize Georgia's natural acidity.

10. Cobb P&D Closeout: Handling the final inspection and filing the completion affidavit to officially update your property's permanent record.

Technical FAQ: Marietta Estate Restoration

How do you protect Marietta's historic driveways during demolition?

Older Marietta driveways often lack the thickness of modern concrete. We utilize low-ground-pressure (LGP) tracks and structural bridging (steel plates or HDPE mats) to ensure the 20-ton load of an excavator is distributed across a massive surface area, preventing cracks or substrate failure.

Can I build a new structure on the old Marietta pool site?

Yes, but only if you choose our Engineered Full Removal. We remove the entire shell and compact the backfill to 95% Modified Proctor density. We provide the certified testing data required to secure a building permit for an ADU, guest house, or permanent pavilion.

What happens to the existing Marietta pool plumbing?

All plumbing is excavated and capped at the source. We ensure that no abandoned lines are left that could interfere with your property's future irrigation or utility expansions—a critical step for maintaining property value in Cobb County.
