Dunwoody Soil Dynamics & Micaceous Silt Physics

In Dunwoody and across DeKalb County, the primary geotechnical challenge is managing the characteristics of micaceous silt and sandy loam soils. Unlike dense clay or pure sand, micaceous silt contains high concentrations of fine mica flakes. These flat, mineral platelets act like miniature springs within the soil matrix, giving the ground high elasticity and compressibility. If a pool excavation is backfilled with native micaceous silt without precise moisture control, the soil will "sponge" under load. Over time, as groundwater levels rise and fall, the backfill will shift and settle, creating large surface depressions and sinkholes in your yard that can damage landscaping, patios, or new structures.

To overcome this, our engineering protocol utilizes the Standard Proctor Compaction Test (ASTM D698) to determine the soil's Optimum Moisture Content (OMC). Because micaceous silt is highly sensitive to water, we strictly control the moisture levels during the backfilling phase. We place structural fill in thin, horizontal layers (lifts) no deeper than 6 inches. Each lift is compacted using high-impact sheepsfoot and vibratory drum rollers to knead the silt platelets and compress them to a minimum of 95% Modified Proctor density. By carefully managing the moisture-density relationship, we transform elastic silt into a stable, load-bearing foundation that matches the surrounding undisturbed ground, providing a flat and permanent surface for your lawn.

Subterranean Hydrology & The "Bathtub Effect" Mitigation

A major risk during any pool demolition is the accumulation of subsurface water within the old pool shell—a problem known as the "Bathtub Effect." If a gunite or concrete pool is simply filled with dirt without proper drainage, the concrete shell acts as an underground basin. Rainwater seeping through the backfill collects at the bottom of the shell, saturating the soil above it. In Dunwoody's micaceous silt, this continuous saturation turns the backfilled area into a soft, spongy swamp, ruining the turf and creating a structural hazard. The water trapped in this underground basin can also create hydrostatic pressure, damaging nearby retaining walls and house foundations.

Our Dunwoody protocol prevents this through precise hydrogeological fracturing. We use heavy hydraulic hammers to punch 18-inch drainage ports through the concrete pool floor every 4 feet, breaking the impermeable barrier and allowing water to drain naturally into the deeper soil layers. Additionally, we place a 12-to-18-inch base layer of washed #57 stone (crushed granite) at the bottom of the pool shell. This aggregate layer acts as a subterranean drainage channel, guiding water away from the compacted soil and preventing saturation. This engineering-first approach ensures that your yard remains dry and structurally stable through all seasons.

Surface Runoff & Grading Swales in Dunwoody

Removing a large, impermeable pool structure fundamentally changes how rainwater moves across your property. Instead of falling into a pool basin, rainwater now drops onto a soil surface, increasing the potential for erosion and pooling water if the grading is not executed properly. In Dunwoody's hilly areas, uncontrolled runoff can wash away newly planted sod, flood garden beds, and pool near your home's foundation or flow onto your neighbors' lots, violating DeKalb County stormwater ordinances.

Our drainage design resolves these issues before they can start. We design and excavate custom surface swales—shallow, grassy depressions with gentle slopes—that catch runoff and channel it away from your house toward municipal drainage infrastructure or designated landscaping zones. In areas with high groundwater levels or steep grades, we install French drains. These drains consist of perforated pipe buried in a trench of washed crushed granite and wrapped in filter fabric, acting as a subterranean drain to capture and redirect excess water. This total drainage system keeps your yard dry and firm, ensuring your new space remains perfectly functional and safe.
